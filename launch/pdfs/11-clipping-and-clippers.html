Script 11: Clipping and clippers — why they matter

FormatFounder to camera + light B-roll, 70/30 split
Hook styleCuriosity + industry explainer
Use forTop-of-funnel education, audience building, "what is this industry" explainer

[HOOK — 0:00-0:05] To camera, slight lean in. You've heard the word "clipper." But do you know what "clipping" actually is — and why it runs the entire creator economy?

[WHAT IS CLIPPING — 0:05-0:20] Slower, explanatory. Clipping is taking one long-form video — a podcast, a YouTube essay, a livestream — and turning it into ten, twenty, thirty short-form clips. One video in. Dozens of posts out. [B-ROLL: split-screen — long-form on the left, a grid of 8-12 short clips on the right]

[WHO ARE CLIPPERS — 0:20-0:35] Clippers are the people who do it. Freelancers. Agencies. In-house teams at creator studios. They charge fifty bucks to five thousand a clip. There are tens of thousands of them. You've probably never heard of one — but you've watched their work. [B-ROLL: montage of viral clips with small "@clipper_handle" overlays]

[WHY IT MATTERS — 0:35-0:55] Forward, the heart of the video. Here's the thing. A single viral clip can launch a creator's career. A bad clip can bury a brand's launch. The difference is taste — knowing which thirty seconds of a four-hour podcast will actually stop the scroll. That's the whole game.

[RELATIV ANGLE — 0:55-1:10] To camera, energy up. And that's exactly what RelatiV is built for. We don't replace clippers — we make them ten-times faster. Paste a URL, get the three best moments, and a clipper takes it home. The clipper economy is going to be massive. We're building the rails.

[CTA — 1:10-1:15] Direct, still. Link in bio.
