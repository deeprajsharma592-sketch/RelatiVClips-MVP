## THE INTERVENTION (3:00–7:00)
The clipper-account army as a **decentralized distribution network**:

- [ ] **Channel topology of the army**
  - Hundreds of independent TikTok / Shorts accounts posting Adin Ross clip variants
  - Each account operated independently, with its own handle, niche angle, posting cadence
  - No central control, no payout, no permission needed
- [ ] **The mechanics of "weaponization"**
  - Clippers auto-extracted the stream's highest-arousal moments (live reactions, bans, real-money moments, celebrity calls)
  - Edited for **mobile-first vertical** within 5–10 minutes of broadcast
  - Tagged for the algorithm: caption + hashtags + cross-posted
- [ ] **The network effect**
  - The aggregator accounts (top TikTok repost pages) treated individual clippers as a *content supply chain*
  - Clip-of-a-clip re-edits became their own content tier
  - Adin Ross himself occasionally reacted, which looped the network back to source
- [ ] **Why it beat paid media**
  - The clipper army had **algorithmic independence** — no single point of failure, no single platform policy takedown could kill the distribution
  - The clips were *unpaid*, so the marginal cost of virality was zero
  - The "creator-as-content-machine" framing didn't apply — the content was *about* the creator, generated by the network
