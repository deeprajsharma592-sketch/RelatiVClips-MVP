## THE BOTTLENECK (0:30–3:00)
- The industry assumption c. 2017–2020: sustained relevance requires **release cadence + mainstream media access + tour visibility**.
- Carti's position against that assumption:
  - Sparse, delayed releases (*Die Lit* 2018 → *Whole Lotta Red* 2020, with long silences between)
  - Minimal press — no late-night, minimal features, no full interviews
  - Visual identity is **deliberately opaque** — masks, distorted vocals, cryptic snippets
- Why conventional marketing would have failed him: the persona *is* the absence. A normal press cycle would have broken it.

## THE INTERVENTION (3:00–7:00)
The fan-led clipping network as a *distributed marketing apparatus*. Cover each mechanism with concrete examples:

- [ ] **Leaked-snippet economy**
  - Pre-release audio surfaced on SoundCloud / YouTube / Twitter / Discord servers
  - Fans treated leaks as **promo material**, not theft
  - The leak → reaction → mainstream pickup loop
- [ ] **Fan-edit ecosystem**
  - Slowed + reverb edits (the "vibe" economy) on SoundCloud, then TikTok
  - Side-by-side comparisons, mosh-pit edits, Photoshopped cover art
  - Re-cut music videos from raw footage
- [ ] **Channel topology**
  - SoundCloud aggregators → Twitter thread curators → TikTok remix accounts → YouTube reaction channels
  - Each tier added distribution surface, none were centrally controlled
- [ ] **The "Opium" multiplier**
  - The label/collective as a *brand surface* for fan creators, not a top-down distributor
  - Fan merch, fan edits posted to Opium-adjacent hashtags, mutual amplification
- [ ] **The RelatiV mirror**
  - *"This is the same pipeline RelatiV automates: ingest raw long-form → identify the hook moments → distribute with per-platform optimization. Carti's fans did it manually for a decade."*
